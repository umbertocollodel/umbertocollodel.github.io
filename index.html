About

I am a macroeconomist specializing in monetary policy, forecasting, and macro-financial linkages. My research combines traditional empirical methods with modern machine learning approaches, particularly Large Language Models, to analyze central bank communication and develop improved forecasting tools.

I have research experience at the Paris School of Economics, International Monetary Fund, OECD, and Central Bank of Malta.

Research Interests: Monetary Policy Communication • Forecasting • LLMs in Economics • Financial Crises • Capital Flows

Job Market Paper

Job Market Paper
Interpreting the Interpreter: Can We Model post-ECB Conferences Volatility with LLM Agents?

This paper investigates whether Large Language Model agents can capture market volatility following ECB press conferences, offering new insights into the information content of central bank communication. Using advanced natural language processing techniques, I demonstrate how AI can parse nuanced monetary policy signals that drive financial market reactions.
